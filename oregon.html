The 7 Wonders
of Oregon

Home to Adventure

Wonder #1

The Columbia
River Gorge

The Columbia River is the largest river in the Pacific Northwest of North America

Over 10+ waterfalls and hiking trails within an hour drive of Portland

Amazing viewpoints that require some time driving on the wonderful I-84 highway

Wonder #2

Oregon Coast

Trails that show the true beauty of the Pacific Ocean

The view is beyond words, the sun setting on the ocean, the calm and relaxing vibe

Wake up early to enjoy the beautiful sunrise and catch calms and crabs with low tides

Wonder #3

Smith Rock
State Park

Located in the high deserts of Oregon, but this 641 acres of land has all sorts of weathers

Misery Ridge Trail is one of the most beautiful trails at Smith Rock, being able to see above the horizons

Huckle berry is native to the Pacific Northwest and a must have at the local ice crea shop outside the entrance of Smith Rock

Wonder #4

Crater Lake

Crater Lake National Park is home to the deepest lake in the United States

Travel the 30 miles of wonderous viewing of the lake and nature

Crater Lake was not always a lake, but started off as a volcano that erupted centuries ago

Wonder #5

Mt. Hood

Mt. Hood is Oregon's highest peak and one of the most prominent of the Cascades volcanoes

Mt. Hood National Forest has wonderful views, hot springs, trails, lakes and even camping spots

Take your winter trip adventures to the mountains between November to March annually
